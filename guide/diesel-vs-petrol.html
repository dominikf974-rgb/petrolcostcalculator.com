The break-even idea (no heavy maths)

Diesel can be more efficient, but depending on your situation it may come with higher fixed costs. Break-even means your fuel savings outweigh any extra yearly costs.

Annual fuel saving = (annual km ÷ 100) × (petrol cost/100km − diesel cost/100km)

FAQ

Is diesel always cheaper than petrol?

No. It depends on consumption and the price per litre. Compare both as cost per 100km to see the real difference.

Is diesel always more fuel-efficient?

Often yes, but real-world driving (speed, weather, load, short trips) can reduce the gap.

How do I decide quickly?

Calculate cost per 100km for both and multiply by your annual mileage. Then consider any fixed-cost differences relevant to your situation.

How do I split costs if we travel together?

Calculate total trip fuel cost, then split per person or per distance ridden.
