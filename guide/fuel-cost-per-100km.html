Fuel cost per 100km

Quick formula: Fuel consumption (L/100km) × Fuel price (per litre) = cost per 100km.

Why cost per 100km is so useful

“L/100km” tells you efficiency, but cost per 100km tells you what you’ll pay. It’s perfect for comparing cars, fuel types, and routes—especially when prices change.

  • Compare diesel vs petrol in real money
  • Estimate commute and monthly spend
  • Convert quickly to any trip distance

The formula

Cost per 100km = Consumption (L/100km) × Price (per litre)

Example (petrol)

6.8 L/100km × 1.85 per litre = 12.58 per 100km

Example (diesel)

5.5 L/100km × 1.75 per litre = 9.63 per 100km

Convert cost per 100km to any trip

Trip cost = (Trip distance ÷ 100) × Cost per 100km

Example: 240km at 12.58/100km → (240 ÷ 100) × 12.58 = 30.19

Common mistakes

  • Using unrealistic consumption: real-world driving differs from official figures.
  • Mixing units: make sure it’s L/100km (not km/L).
  • Cherry-picking fuel price: use the price you actually pay.

Tips to reduce fuel cost per 100km

  • Check tyre pressure
  • Reduce cruising speed
  • Drive smoothly and avoid hard braking
  • Remove unnecessary weight and roof boxes when possible

FAQ

How do I calculate fuel cost per 100km?

Multiply your consumption (L/100km) by the fuel price per litre.

What is a good cost per 100km?

It depends on fuel price, vehicle, and driving conditions. Use the formula to compare your real-world costs.

Can I estimate monthly fuel costs from this?

Yes: (monthly km ÷ 100) × cost per 100km.

Why does my real cost differ from official figures?

Weather, speed, load, tyre choice, and short trips can all increase real-world consumption.
