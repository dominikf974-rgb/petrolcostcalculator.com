10 fuel-saving tips that actually work

  1. Lower cruising speed: aerodynamic drag rises quickly at higher speeds.
  2. Check tyre pressure: do it before you leave (cold tyres).
  3. Drive smoothly: avoid rapid acceleration and late braking.
  4. Reduce drag: remove roof boxes/racks when not needed.
  5. Pack light: unnecessary weight costs fuel.
  6. Plan refuelling: avoid “forced” stops at expensive stations.
  7. Use AC wisely: extreme cooling increases fuel use—aim for moderate settings.
  8. Choose efficient routes: fewer stops and less congestion can save fuel.
  9. Avoid idling: modern engines don’t need long warm-ups.
  10. Share the ride: splitting costs makes the trip cheaper per person.

FAQ

What saves the most fuel on a road trip?

Often: reducing speed and driving smoothly. Tyre pressure and removing roof drag can also make a noticeable difference.

Should I use air conditioning or open windows?

At higher speeds, open windows increase drag. Moderate AC can be more efficient on motorways.

How do I estimate fuel cost for a long trip?

Use the calculator with distance, consumption, and fuel price—or calculate cost per 100km and scale it to your distance.

How do we split fuel costs on a road trip?

Calculate the total fuel cost and split per person (or by distance ridden if someone joins only part-way).
