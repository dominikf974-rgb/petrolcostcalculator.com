How to calculate fuel costs (simple)

For a quick estimate, you only need three values: distance, fuel consumption and fuel price. This calculator also shows cost per 100 km and cost per person.

Quick formulas

  • Litres = distance × consumption ÷ 100
  • Fuel cost = litres × price
  • Per person = total cost ÷ passengers

Good to know

  • Tolls are a rough regional estimate.
  • “Running costs” are a simplified approximation.
  • For best accuracy, use real consumption + current price.

Tips to save fuel & reduce trip costs

Small changes can reduce your trip cost fast: correct tyre pressure, steady speed, and realistic consumption values. If you travel with friends, splitting costs fairly avoids arguments and makes planning easier.

  • Drive smoother: avoid hard acceleration and braking.
  • Check tyre pressure: low pressure increases consumption.
  • Remove heavy load: weight increases fuel usage.
  • Split costs: use “Passengers” to calculate per-person cost.

Quick FAQ

How do I calculate fuel cost per 100 km?

The calculator shows $/100 km automatically. Formula: total cost ÷ distance × 100.

Should I enter one-way or round trip distance?

Either works. Enter the kilometres you will actually drive (one-way or round trip).

What is included in “running costs”?

A simple estimate (maintenance, tyres, depreciation, insurance) plus the toll estimate per 100 km.

How accurate are toll estimates?

Tolls are a rough estimate by region. Actual tolls depend on route, vehicle class and local rules.
