Fuel Cost Calculator Guide: How to Estimate Your Real Trip Cost

← Back to the calculator

Most people only calculate the fuel price — but the real cost of driving can be higher when you include maintenance, depreciation, tyres, insurance and (in many countries) tolls. This guide shows you the simple formula, real examples, and how to compare “cost per mile”.

1) Fuel cost formula (simple)

The basic fuel-only calculation is:

Formula:
(Distance × Consumption ÷ 100) × Fuel price

Quick example

300 km, 7.0 L/100km, fuel price €1.90:
(300 × 7.0 ÷ 100) × 1.90 = €39.90

2) Examples: 100 / 300 / 600 miles

Here are common distances. For simplicity, we’ll use 7.5 L/100km and €1.90/L. (If you use MPG + price per gallon, the calculator handles that too if your EN version supports it.)

100 miles (~161 km)
Litres: 161 × 7.5 ÷ 100 = 12.1 L
Fuel cost: 12.1 × 1.90 = €23.0 (approx.)
300 miles (~483 km)
Litres: 483 × 7.5 ÷ 100 = 36.2 L
Fuel cost: 36.2 × 1.90 = €68.8 (approx.)
600 miles (~966 km)
Litres: 966 × 7.5 ÷ 100 = 72.5 L
Fuel cost: 72.5 × 1.90 = €137.8 (approx.)

3) Cost per mile

“Cost per mile” is perfect for comparing options (driving vs train, taking a different car, carpooling, etc.). Use: Total trip cost ÷ distance.

Example: $120 total for 600 miles → 120 ÷ 600 = $0.20 per mile

4) Why “real driving cost” is higher

Fuel is only one part. The biggest hidden costs are:

  • Depreciation: cars lose value with every mile
  • Maintenance & repairs: servicing, brakes, oil, inspections
  • Tyres: wear depends on driving style and vehicle
  • Insurance & taxes: fixed yearly costs
  • Parking/tolls: common in cities and highways
Practical tip: If your calculator includes “running cost” estimation, use it to get a closer real-world figure.

5) Tolls & road charges (basic estimate)

Tolls depend on route, country/state, and vehicle class. Even a rough estimate helps avoid surprises. If your calculator supports route-based toll estimation, enter start and destination and compare.

6) Easy ways to save fuel

  • Tyre pressure: low pressure increases consumption
  • Smooth driving: hard acceleration burns fuel
  • Remove weight: don’t carry unnecessary items
  • Shop fuel prices: stations can vary a lot
  • Carpool: lower cost per person immediately

7) Quick FAQ

Why does my result differ from other calculators?

Because fuel price, real consumption, route, and extra costs vary. Your true average consumption is the key input.

Which consumption number should I use?

Use your real average from your car or app. Manufacturer ratings are often optimistic.

Can I calculate electric car charging cost?

This guide focuses on fuel. An EV charging calculator is a great next tool (and often ranks well).
